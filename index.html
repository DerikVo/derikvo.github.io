Derik Vo

Aspiring Educator | Data Scientist | Lifelong Learner

My passion for education began with early childhood development and the study of social sciences. After finishing my undergraduate degree, I became interested in research and developed analytics skills. Shortly after graduating, I worked for a now-defunct program designed to help high school students explore college and vocational school options. Eventually, I transitioned into data science, machine learning, and generative artificial intelligence. During this time, I taught analytics to underrepresented and underserved college graduates who were under- or unemployed. Teaching these students reignited my love for education and led me to pivot back into the field. I hope to apply the skills I’ve developed over the years to make data-informed decisions that create equitable and nurturing learning environments.

My Philosophy of Education

"Learning is a result of listening, which in turn leads to even better listening and attentiveness to the other person. In other words, to learn from the child, we must have empathy, and empathy grows as we learn."
- Alice Miller

My Beliefs About Equity in the Classroom

“The research is abundantly clear; nothing motivates a child more than when learning is valued by schools and families/community working together in partnership…. These forms of [parent] involvement do not happen by accident or even by invitation. They happen by explicit strategic intervention.”
- Michael Fullan
